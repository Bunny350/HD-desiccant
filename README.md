# HD desiccant holder

*The best calibration model since everything else.*

<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Bunny350/HD-desiccant/main/Media/Images/hd-desiccant-banger-render.png" height="250px" />

This desiccant holder / container is called HD desiccant holder because it holds any type of desiccant *in diameters that are within 3mm to 5mm*. It has 2mm wide grills that improves drying performance when being dried by normal filament dryers. Becuase of this, salvaging desiccant bags for this is not recommended as they contain pellets that are smaller than the holder's grill.

It is also a great printer / filament torture test as it tests every aspect of the printer's tolerances and mostly filament retraction.

<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Bunny350/HD-desiccant/main/Media/Images/IMG_3950.jpg" height="250px" />

Since I don't like other desiccants for their strict tolerances **(after printing)** and the outcome of it's smaller pellet compatibility with poorer drying performance, I decided to make it myself. Heavily relied on thin extrude feature that introduced four years ago, and simple locking mechanism.

[Snappy sounds demo (Instagram)](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DVqocWHkwL2/)

## Recommended materials
* ABS
* ASA

* PET-G
    * May be minorly distortied by heat under high heat mode. However this does not affect the performance.
    
* PC
    * It requires excessive force to lock initially, at least V0.2378 and Bambu PC.
    
* FDM / FFF PA
    * It may lead to worse tolerances. It's probably one of the materials that are hard to tune.

* Just don't make it via PLA or PVB as these will have insanely obvious problems, unless it says it can tolerate high temperatures (something like Polymaker HT-PLA).

Any other materials, such as SLA photoplastics are also welcome, so we can torture more types of printers.

## Requirements
* The printer and the material must handle 0.2mm tolerances.
    * If the printer cannot handle 0.2mm tolerances, including but not limited to the lid not slotting into the bottom holder, then try drying the material first, before adjusting on the slicer- and printer-side.
    
* Easily-configurable parametric model requires either Fusion or online connection and MakerWorld.
* [Starter packs (drag-drop & YOLO print) is exclusive to MakerWorld.](https://makerworld.com/models/2565724-hd-desiccant-holder#profileId-2827543)

## Assembly

<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Bunny350/HD-desiccant/main/Media/Images/IMG_3953.GIF" height="250px" />

* Carefully fill the desiccant holder (desiccant_bottom_casing) using the desiccant of your choice.

<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Bunny350/HD-desiccant/main/Media/Assembly%20images/hd_desiccant_covering.png" height="250px" />
<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Bunny350/HD-desiccant/main/Media/Assembly%20images/hd_desiccant_locking.png" height="250px" />

*Note: these images are from the early prototype.*

* Close the desiccant holder with the lid (desiccant_top_lid). Turn the lid clockwise to lock it. It may make a click.

## Disassembly
If HD desiccant did not dry properly, or for whatever reason, you can easily disassemble it, as easy as assembling it.

<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Bunny350/HD-desiccant/main/Media/Assembly%20images/hd_desiccant_locking.png" height="250px" />
<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Bunny350/HD-desiccant/main/Media/Assembly%20images/hd_desiccant_covering.png" height="250px" />

*Note: these images are from the early prototype.*

* Turn the lid counter-clockwise, and then the lid will extend on its own. You can carefully pull the lid if it can't be extended on its own.
* The desiccant can be removed and rejuvenated without the holder.

## Drying
Desiccant in HD desiccant holder can be rejuvenated / dried within minimum setting of medium heat (around 60˚C) and 6 hours.
